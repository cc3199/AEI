Mission Statement

Science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) skills and knowledge form a foundation for progress and prosperity. The creativity, capability and potential of African students in these areas must not be hindered by a lack of resources. The African Einstein Initiative aims to promote and support efforts to provide, improve, or enhance quality pre-college education in sub-Saharan Africa with an emphasis on STEM education.
To provide families and students with resources necessary to obtain education, to facilitate gender equity related to quality education, to partner with African educators to improve STEM education, to provide the necessary tools and materials for effective teaching of STEM, to build and equip technology/STEM resource centers in schools, and to establish well supported Intel ISEF affiliated science fairs- as well as the Conrad Challenge, starting in Ghana, West Africa.
